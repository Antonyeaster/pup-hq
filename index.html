Why puppy training is so important?

Anxiety

Dog anxiety has become a huge factor in this day and age. People working from home and being with your dog is great... Unitil, you go out and your dog suddenly starts losing its mind.

To counter this a good habit would be to have a crate, which will allow you to create that alone feeling for your puppy while you are at home. Then, when you leave the house over time your puppy will become used to being alone and it will be a much smalled shock to the system.

Sleep/Rest

When it comes to sleep and rest it's really important that you give your puppy and good amount of it. After a walk put your puppy in a crate to rest. After playing put your puppy in the crate to rest. After food can be another good time to put your puppy in the crate to rest.
This will help in a couple of different ways, it will teach them that the crate is not a bad place to go, it's a place they can rest and calm down. It will help with the snappy biting, this usually happens when a puppy is over tired.
Puppies are like babies, it may sound obvious but its often over looked.

Toilet Training

Tired of finding puddles around your house?
Before you go out and buy puppy training pads, think about what you are teaching your puppy when you use them. You are teaching them it's ok to go to the toilet in the house, this really is the wrong approach. Instead, you need to be on the ball, take them outside frequently and praise them when they supply the goods. Remember, if your puppy goes to the toilet in the house, its your fault for not letting them outside often enough. You shouldn't be telling you puppy off, you should have a word with yourself and learn from it.
